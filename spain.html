Part of my time is spent in Galicia, where I am working on the renovation of a derelict stone farmhouse. A personal project — and a direct introduction to the Galician building culture.

Background

Why Galicia

I have been travelling to Galicia for several years — initially drawn by the landscape and the architecture, then by the opportunity to buy and work on a building of my own. In 2025 I purchased a derelict two-storey granite farmhouse in Negreira, A Coruña. It was partially collapsed internally, the floor structure failed, and the site was completely overgrown.

The renovation is ongoing. Working through it — planning permissions, local contractors, traditional lime mortars, the aparejador process — has given me a practical working knowledge of the Galician building system that is difficult to get any other way.

Alongside my own project, I work with a small number of international buyers who are navigating the same process: identifying a property, understanding what they can and cannot do with it, and getting a project started in an unfamiliar system.

The project

Casa Negreira

A two-storey granite rubble masonry building on the edge of the town of Negreira — partially collapsed internally when purchased. The restoration is being carried out in phases, working with local contractors and traditional materials.

The approach is restrained: exposed stone walls cleaned and repointed in lime, handmade terracotta tiles on the ground floor, a double-height volume created by opening the original floor structure to the roof ridge.

  • LocationNegreira, A Coruña, Galicia
  • Building typeGranite rubble masonry, two storeys
  • StatusRenovation in progress
  • ApproachLime mortar, exposed stone, terracotta floors, traditional materials
  • PlanningLicencia de obras — navigating the Spanish system directly
