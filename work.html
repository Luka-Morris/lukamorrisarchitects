01

4 Beverley Road

Residential refurbishment & extension · Barnes, SW13 · Robert Barnes Architects

A major refurbishment and partial rebuild of a semi-detached house in Barnes. The ground floor was extended and entirely reorganised to create a continuous open living space for the whole family — kitchen, dining, and sitting zones defined by ceiling changes and large rooflights rather than walls.

A polished concrete floor runs uninterrupted from inside to garden, dissolving the threshold between the two. The project involved complex party wall negotiations with three adjoining neighbours and the introduction of a structural steel frame to achieve the open plan. High-end kitchen and bathroom specification throughout.

The upper floors were retained largely as existing, concentrating the intervention — and the budget — where it would have the most impact on daily family life.

02

24 Florian Road

Residential refurbishment & extension · Putney, SW15 · Robert Barnes Architects

A whole-house refurbishment of a terraced property in Putney, sandwiched between a main road and an active tube line — a constraint that became the project's defining idea. The rear yard was enclosed by a new double-glazed glass box, creating a quiet, light-filled kitchen and living space that renders the railway invisible from within.

A narrow slot at the edge of the box accommodates a living green wall, providing biodiversity and herbs for a family who cook seriously. The glass box is visible from passing tube trains — a considered exchange between public infrastructure and private domesticity.

All original kitchen furniture from the client's previous home — cherry oak units — was disassembled and recut into new bespoke joinery pieces: a chair, a tallboy, fitted storage, and shelving, each designed to work within the new rooms. Nothing was wasted. The project achieves MVHR ventilation, photovoltaic panels, battery storage, and an air source heat pump, with monitoring estimating 70% renewable energy self-sufficiency.

With Robert Barnes Architects, London. Bespoke furniture designed in collaboration with the client.

03

22 Hill Street

Commercial heritage refurbishment · Mayfair, W1J · Emrys Architects

A complete refurbishment of a six-storey Georgian townhouse in Mayfair for a new private client, targeting BREEAM Excellent — a rare and technically demanding certification for a listed building in a conservation area. The project required sensitive re-planning across CAT A shell-and-core and CAT B fit-out, working within strict heritage constraints while introducing contemporary services, raised access floors, and acoustic separation between tenancies.

The main staircase was extended to the roof level with a new glazed panel above, drawing borrowed daylight deep into the historic stair core. The existing ornate balustrade, chandelier, and herringbone carpet runner were carefully retained, restated, and integrated into the new design. Bespoke timber shutters to all Georgian sash windows were drawn at detail level and fabricated by specialist joinery.

A new courtyard at lower ground floor level was designed as a landscaped outdoor room accessible from multiple tenancies. A roof terrace at the upper levels adds amenity space with views across Mayfair.

With Emrys Architects, London. Images include visualisations produced during the design process alongside construction photography.
